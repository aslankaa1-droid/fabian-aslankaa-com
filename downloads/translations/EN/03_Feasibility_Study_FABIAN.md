## 1. TECHNICAL AND ECONOMIC CHARACTERISTICS OF THE PROJECT

### 1.1. Substance of the Project

The FABIAN project creates a premium trademark of still table water for the HoReCa channel in the CIS countries, with a hybrid development model: a direct pilot in Moscow and Saint Petersburg in Y1–Y2, scaling via a network of bottling franchisees from Y3, and a global expansion through the JV in DMCC in Y4–Y5.

### 1.2. Key Project Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Planning horizon | 5 years (Y1–Y5) |
| Discount rate | 18% |
| Base accounting currency | RUB (Russian contour), USD (JV contour) |
| FX rate used | RUB 92 / USD 1 |
| Production model Y1–Y2 | Contract bottling |
| Production model Y3+ | Contract + franchise |
| Initial investment window | $4.5M |

## 5. BREAK-EVEN POINT

### 5.1. BEP Calculation (Y2, full ramp-up benchmark)

**Fixed costs (FC) Y2:** RUB 780M.
**Variable margin per bottle (weighted average):** RUB 88 (price RUB 145 − COGS RUB 57).
**BEP in bottles, Y2:** 780,000,000 / 88 ≈ **8.86M bottles.**
**BEP in revenue, Y2:** ≈ RUB 1,285M.

Actual Y2 volume = 11.5M bottles; safety margin ≈ 23%.

### 5.2. BEP by Year

| Parameter | Y1 | Y2 | Y3 | Y4 | Y5 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| FC, RUB M | 438 | 780 | 1,295 | 1,760 | 2,200 |
| VM per bottle, RUB | 86 | 88 | 90 | 92 | 94 |
| BEP, M bottles | 5.1 | 8.9 | 14.4 | 19.1 | 23.4 |
| Actual, M bottles | 4.2 | 11.5 | 26.0 | 44.0 | 62.0 |
| Safety margin | (17%) | 23% | 45% | 57% | 62% |

In Y1 the project runs below BEP — a normal phenomenon for the pilot phase; the loss is covered by the investment round.

## 9. FEASIBILITY CONCLUSIONS

1. The project is economically viable: NPV @ 18% = $11.3M, IRR = 38%, DPP ≈ 3.2 years (discounted).
2. Five-year CapEx = RUB 727M ($7.9M), of which $4.5M is covered by the investment round.
3. Sensitivity to key factors is robust: positive NPV is preserved in all key scenarios except the extreme combination.
4. Socio-economic impact — 1,100+ jobs by Y5 and RUB 3.6 billion in taxes over 5 years.
5. The principal operational risk — contract bottler failure — is mitigated by a second contract and batch audit.
