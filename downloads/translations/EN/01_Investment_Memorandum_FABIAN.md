## 2. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### 2.1. The Opportunity in One Paragraph

FABIAN is a premium still table water brand bottled in glass and designed for the HoReCa channel (restaurants, hotels, premium catering) in the CIS market, with Moscow and Saint Petersburg as the launch geography. The project is built on three windows that have opened simultaneously:

1. **Withdrawal of international premium brands** (Evian, Perrier, S.Pellegrino, Acqua Panna) from Russian HoReCa shelves between 2022 and 2025 has left a segment worth more than RUB 18 billion per year unattended.
2. **The ban on PET packaging in Moscow HoReCa from 1 September 2025** makes glass not "a choice" but the only permitted format in the premium segment.
3. **A trademark registered in 2018** (FABIAN), design rights (LenaMcCoder) and Halal certification from day one provide a defensible barrier to entry.

Round requested: **$4–5M (Series A)** — pilot in Moscow and Saint Petersburg; an optional $8M activates an accelerated scenario with a simultaneous launch in four cities and a proprietary bottling line.

### 5.3. Why the Window Is Open — Why Now

**Trigger 1 — withdrawal of international brands (2022–2025):**
- Evian (Danone) — material supply contraction to Russian HoReCa since Q2 2022.
- Perrier (Nestlé Waters) — same.
- S.Pellegrino + Acqua Panna (Nestlé Sanpellegrino) — same.
- Voss — irregular parallel-import supplies, 220–280% premium over pre-war price.

**Trigger 2 — regulatory pressure on PET:**
- Decree of the Government of Moscow — ban on single-use PET packaging in HoReCa from 1 September 2025 (drinking water included).
- Anticipated extension to Saint Petersburg (draft decree expected Q1 2026).
- Pilot programmes in Sochi and Kazan.

**Trigger 3 — premium-tier import substitution:**
- Government procurement (Administrative Directorate of the President, Federation Council, regional administrations) — transition to domestic premium SKUs.
- Corporate segment (banks, oil & gas, IT) — internal import-substitution policies for VIP zones, meeting rooms and catering.

**Trigger 4 — the halal segment:**
- 25+ million Muslim consumers in Russia.
- Only 12% of premium water SKUs hold a valid Halal certificate (Roshalal, 2025).
- A growing inflow of halal-aware tourists from the Middle East to Moscow, Saint Petersburg and the Caucasian Mineral Waters region.

## 7. BUSINESS MODEL

### 7.1. Hybrid Model (Contract Bottling → Own Line → Franchise)

**Phase 1 (Y1–Y2): contract bottling (asset-light).**
- Partner bottler in the Moscow region (well source plus bottling line).
- FABIAN manages: brand, design, distribution, B2B sales, marketing.
- CapEx: only glass moulds (~$0.8M) and starting inventory.

**Phase 2 (Y2–Y3): own bottling line in the Moscow cluster.**
- Acquisition or long-term lease of a glass bottling line (capacity 6,000 bottles/hour).
- CapEx: $3.2M (covers SKUs 0.5L + 0.75L + 1.0L).
- Funding: Series B or debt financing against revenue.

**Phase 3 (Y3–Y5): franchise regional bottling model.**
- In every major CIS region (Almaty, Tashkent, Minsk, Tbilisi, Baku) — a licensed partner bottler with a local source under the FABIAN brand.
- Royalty: 6–8% of franchisee Net Revenue plus bottle-mould leasing.

### 7.2. Sales Channels

**Direct B2B HoReCa (60% of revenue in Y3):**
- Chain hotels 4–5 stars (Aman, Four Seasons, Mandarin Oriental, Lotte, Metropol, Kempinski).
- Premium restaurants (Novikov Group, White Rabbit Family, AB Restaurants, Arkady Novikov, Twins Garden).
- Premium catering (Catering-Bureau, Figaro Catering).
- VIP zones of corporate and government customers.

**B2B distribution (25% in Y3):**
- 3 key HoReCa distributors in MSK + SPB (Prostor, BarPromSnab, Kulineria).

**Premium retail (12% in Y3):**
- Azbuka Vkusa, Globus Gourmet, Zelyony Perekrestok, Metropolis.

**Halal channel (3% in Y3 → 8% in Y5):**
- The Chapan chain (the founder's partner channel).
- Ethnic stores.
