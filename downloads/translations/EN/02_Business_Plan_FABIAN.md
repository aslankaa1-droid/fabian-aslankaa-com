## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

FABIAN is a premium still table water in glass packaging (0.33L and 0.75L formats), engineered for the HoReCa segment of the CIS. The project addresses the structural shortage of locally produced premium-tier glass — a niche traditionally occupied by imports (Evian, Acqua Panna, Fiji), now distorted by sanctions, FX volatility and logistics inflation.

**Business model — hybrid, in three phases:**

- **Phase 1 (Y1–Y2):** direct pilot in Moscow and Saint Petersburg. Contract bottling, focus on the top 300 restaurants and 4–5 star hotels. Investment round $4–5M.
- **Phase 2 (Y3):** strategic fork. Based on the pilot, scale via bottling franchise by Russian federal district and CIS country.
- **Phase 3 (Y4–Y5):** global expansion through the JV hub in DMCC (UAE), targeting the Middle East, Southeast Asia and premium island resorts.

**Legal contour:**
- JV in DMCC (Dubai) — holder of IP, manager of export flows, recipient of payments from CIS and overseas franchisees.
- Russian operating company — production, sales, logistics, standard Russian tax regime.

**Key Y5 financial indicators (Base scenario):**

| EBITDA margin, Y5 | 22.6% |
| Payback period | 3.7 years |

## 3. MARKET ANALYSIS

### 3.1. CIS Market Size

The CIS retail bottled water market is estimated at RUB 320–360 billion (~$3.8 billion), growing at 5–7% per annum. The premium segment (glass, shelf price above RUB 200 per 0.75L) accounts for about 4% by volume and 12–14% by value of the market, that is RUB 38–46 billion. The HoReCa channel within premium glass is RUB 14–18 billion, with pronounced concentration in Moscow (~48%), Saint Petersburg (~14%) and other million-plus cities.

### 3.2. HoReCa Segment

Target audience for FABIAN at the pilot stage:

- **Class A — fine dining and starred restaurants:** ~380 venues in MSK + SPB. Water consumption: 2–3 bottles of 0.75L per dinner, restaurant mark-up 380–600%.
- **Class B — premium chain restaurants and gastrobars:** ~1,100 venues.
- **Class C — hotels 4–5 stars:** ~220 properties with banquet service, mini-bar and room service.
- **Class D — premium private events / catering:** estimated 60–80 operators.

### 3.3. Trends

- The withdrawal of international brands and the inflation of imports (Evian +120% versus 2021) opened a price window of RUB 280–420 per 0.75L on the HoReCa shelf.
- Restaurant demand for "a local story with a European code" — a clear trend since 2023.
- Growth of water sommelier programmes (water pairing) — present in 28% of top Moscow restaurants.
- Glass as a mandatory premium attribute: 92% of the top-100 restaurants remove PET water from their menu.

### 3.4. Competitive Map

| Category | Players | Strengths | Vulnerabilities |
|---|---|---|---|
| International premium | Evian, Acqua Panna, S. Pellegrino | Brand, heritage | Price, logistics, sanctions risk |
| Russian premium | Arkhyz Selection, Chernogolovka Glass, Senezhskaya | Recognition | No coherent HoReCa positioning |
| Regional glass SKUs | Local mineral waters | Cheaper | Irregular supply, non-premium packaging |
| Grey-channel imports | Parallel imports | Range | Price, supply gaps |

**FABIAN's competitive wedge:** glass + design + sommelier programme + stable HoReCa service + price 18–25% below the Evian-equivalent.

## 4. PRODUCT

### 4.1. SKUs

| SKU | Volume | Packaging | Target channel | Recommended restaurant price |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| FABIAN Still 0.33 | 0.33 L | Glass, screw cap | Room service, banquet | RUB 220–280 |
| FABIAN Still 0.75 | 0.75 L | Glass, premium swing cap | Restaurant table | RUB 380–520 |

### 4.2. Technical Specification Parameters

- Category: still drinking table water.
- Mineralisation: 150–350 mg/L (low-mid).
- pH: 7.2–7.8.
- Source: artesian well with confirmed compositional stability over 24+ months.
- Certification: Customs Union Technical Regulation 021/2011, EAEU 044/2017, ISO 22000 at the contract bottler.

### 4.3. Packaging and Design

- Glass: flint, wall thickness 2.8–3.2 mm; 0.75L bottle weight 480–520 g.
- Label: premium uncoated paper with embossing, runs from 50,000 units.
- Cap: aluminium swing cap for 0.75L, metal screw cap for 0.33L.
- Transport packaging: corrugated cardboard, 12 units (0.75L) and 24 units (0.33L), RPET dividers.

### 4.4. Assortment Strategy

In Y1–Y2 — two still SKUs. In Y3 — extension to sparkling 0.75L; in Y4 — premium glass 1.0L for the banquet channel, plus limited editions for events.

## 6. OPERATING MODEL

### 6.1. Contract Bottling (Y1–Y2)

In Phase 1 no proprietary production is built. FABIAN uses contract bottling at certified facilities in the Central region with available glass lines:

- Minimum order lot: 200,000 bottles.
- Schedule: 4–5 production cycles per year in Y1, monthly from Y2.
- Quality control: incoming water control plus laboratory audit of each batch before dispatch.

### 6.2. Logistics and Warehousing

- **Central warehouse Moscow** — 1,200 sqm, leased, 3PL.
- **Saint Petersburg satellite** — 350 sqm from Y2.
- **HoReCa delivery:** night windows (22:00–07:00), fleet of 4 light trucks (3PL).

### 6.3. Procurement

- Glass: 2 Russian suppliers (diversification).
- Label: 1 premium-print supplier, 1 backup.
- Caps: 1 supplier with 6-month safety stock.
- Cardboard: 2 suppliers.

### 6.4. Quality

Mandatory laboratory monitoring of each batch across 24 parameters (microbiology, ionic composition, organoleptics). Arbitration samples retained for 18 months.
